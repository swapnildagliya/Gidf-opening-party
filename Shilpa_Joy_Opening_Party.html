About you
A Bharatanatyam artist based in the Netherlands, Shilpa Joy began her journey in the art form at the age of four — a practice that has since grown both into a discipline and a deeply personal way of understanding herself and the world. She trained under Guru Kalamandalam Prasheeja Gopinadh during her formative years, receiving early exposure to Bharatanatyam and Mohiniyattam and performing in traditional settings. She currently continues her Bharatanatyam training under Guru Shyama Warrier at Natanam Academy, Netherlands, working within a Kalakshetra-influenced approach. She has performed across various cultural platforms in the Netherlands, including Korzo's Colours of India, as well as events in Amsterdam, Eindhoven and Veldhoven, alongside regular presentations at Natanam Academy. At the heart of her practice is a desire to make the internal visible: to translate feeling, character and meaning into movement that resonates. Bharatanatyam, for her, is also a form of grounding, a source of joy and a way of creating quiet shared moments between performer and audience.
